关卡布局与灯光使用技巧

发表于2024-05-21|更新于2024-10-29|游戏设计

笔记

演讲分享了如何使用关卡布局(layout)来自然地引导玩家，从而最大限度地减少HUD标记(HUD Marks)和强制摄像机移动(Forced Camera Moves)的依赖。
原视频链接：Level Design Workshop Blockmesh and Lighting Tips

Blockmesh Guidance(白盒布局的引导)

Block = Greybox = Whitebox, etc

Affordance

  • properties of an object that define its possible uses or make clear it can or should be used

功能可见性：物体或环境展现出的功能关卡中的一切交互、沟通、引导等。通风口是否可以跳下、绳子是否可以攀爬、门是否可以推动等等。 也即关卡中涉及的环境机制, 以及这些机制与玩家的互动。充分的功能可见性会与关卡可读性呈正相关。

  • A way to communicate to the player what to play with or where to go
  • Player learn the affordance rules via consistent color and shape and trust contract is formed(form Vacabulary)

Denying Affordance

  • Communicates to the player where NOT to go
  • Contextualize why the player can’t use it/go there.

Vistual Language

  • Shape Language

Round – safe
Rectangular – stable and useful
Diagonal – like spine, dangerous, need to look out


  • Color Language

Color consistency – Vacabulary with player, communicates affordance
Color in environmnent – Netural but stand out

Landmarks

  • Orients players
  • Distant object seen form many vantage points in the level
  • A goal to work toward

经常被作为POI使用，也常作为关卡的阶段性目标。充分的辨识度、相互之间无大面积遮挡，在多个角度可以观察到，反复出现。

Optional Attract

  • Caves, doors, archway, etc.
  • Mystery – “What could be inside?”

Gates & Valves

Valves(Single valve)：封锁战斗区域，减少可能性空间，使玩家专注于特定的区域与事件。一些非战斗的单向阀，可以推动剧情的发展，迫使玩家拿到新能力后再利用新能力出来，同时又能对新能力起到教学作用(这种设计在银河恶魔城游戏中很常见)；同时起到对玩家提示的作用：”你走了正确的线路。”

Leading & Lines

  • Lines that draw your eye to the intended point of interest

Pinching

  • Angle shapes to funnel players to a specific spot
  • Good for redirection
  • Great for setting up a reveal – to show POI to player without forcing the camera

隧道、山涧、窄巷、室内狭长的通道……

Framing & Composition

  • Draw attention to point of interest by blocking other parts of image, making it stand out

Breadcrumbs

  • Attract/lead the player, a piece at a time, to a goal
  • Can be almost anything that draws the eye:
    • Stuff that breaks up the negative space of floor/walls
    • Pickups
    • Enemies
    • Lit areas

Textures

  • Point the way to go
    • Arrows pointing the way
    • Scrapes on the ground/wall
    • signs

Movement

  • Use movement to grab the eye where you want players to go or what you want them to see
    • Big scripted moments
    • Birds
    • Spark FX
    • Enemies
    • Something flapping in the breeze

  • Cubves triggered to lerp in a direction or along a spline will suffice
  • Can be a late game bandage for guidance

Light & God Rays

  • Players are attracted to the light
  • God rays draw attention and line

The Squint Test

  • Step1: Squint your eyes and look at the game
  • Step2: Everything will be blurry, but what stands out
  • Step3: Adjust your layout and lighting to hightlight what you want player to focus on

Four Fundamental Light Types

Diectional light, Spotlight, Ambient light, Point light
