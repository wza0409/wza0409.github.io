【札记】Elyot Grant——设计解谜游戏的30堂课

发表于2024-03-26|更新于2024-10-22|游戏设计

虽然视频的标题是”30 Puzzle Design Lessons”，但作者的分享的其实并不是真正的“30节课”，而是系统性地分享了30个谜题设计的话题。我将这30个话题归类到三个大的部分，一是尤里卡时刻(Eureka Moment)；二是谜题的深度(The Depth of the puzzle), 也就是作者所说的”真正传达意义的谜题”；三是余下的具体技巧(Tricks of Puzzle Design)，这些技巧是视频的主要内容。视频主要是站在传统解谜设计的视角，但其中的很多思想和方法同样适用于电子游戏的解谜设计。

原视频链接：Elyot Grant - 30 Puzzle Design Lessons, Extended Director’s Cut
B站搬运中文字幕版：【GDC中英字幕】设计解谜游戏的30堂课 - Elyot Grant（加长版）【SolidFly翻译】

Eureka Moment

  • Eureka Moment – A sudden, pleasureful, fluent, confident feeling of understanding, also known as Aha Monent, Wow Moment, Insights…

  • Sparkle is the “amplitude” of Eureka

  • Eureka is not Fiero.

    • Fiero – Fiero is the emotion of overcoming a tough.Create a puzzle, make it challenging, then player will “feel smart”.It’s a approach motivated by valuing-overcoming the challenge. For example, finally vanquish a really difficult boss.

    So Preserving the ability for player to make the discover on their own which heightens the experience of Fiero.

    • Eureka is to learn the deep interesting thing or making that interesting discovery.

  • Eureka is to discover and comprehend, while Fiero is to solve problem by oneself. Eureka and Fiero mostly overlap during puzzle-solving process, but they are different at all.

Fiero – I figured it out! Any Challenge can confer Fiero.
Eureka – That’s interesting! Only Puzzle can confer Eureka.

The Depth of the puzzle.

  • Normally, we use puzzles for increasing playtime, controlling pacing, inserting an challenge…
  • The asethetic of making a good puzzle isn’t just about making a challenge that makes the player feel satisfied.

“It’s not a good idea to just be trying to make the player feel smart. You want to communicate to them an actual substance of something.”
– Jonathan Blow

  • Make Meaningful puzzling
    • That means do not make puzzles for puzzling.
    • Exploit the natural human puzzle instinct

What to Do in Games

Avoid repetitve and monotonous puzzles.
Combine puzzles with narritation.
Let some puzzles play an significant role in progreesion of the game.

  • Solver Experience
    • Low does vs high dose
    • Low difficulty vs high difficuly
    • Novelty vs repetition
    • Flow vs rollercoaster

Tricks of Puzzle Design

Create a Rewarding Ending

  • Shape makes puzzle interesting while puzzle will depend heavily on the shape being a certain way, which may cause bad puzzle-solving experience.
  • Steps by Steps: show what player want to see –
  • Create setups for metapuzzle(元谜题，谜题的谜底作为另一个谜题的开始，或是解谜的必要条件——循序渐进、分层递进) – A good way to control Pacing and Beats of the level.
  • Exploit Peak-End Rule（峰终定律：用户对一项事物的体验之后,所能记住的就只是在峰(高峰)与终(结束)时的体验,而在过程中好与不好体验的比重、体验时间的长短,对记忆的影响不多。）

Enbed Secrets And Surprises – Enbed more Eureka Momnets in a level

  • “easter eggs”, untracked, missable – additonal excitement

Aporia is a Promise of Surprise

  • Aporia – A state of puzzlement where the solver believes that the puzzle is impossible（like many topology puzzles）
  • Aporia creates a promise:
    • “A interesting trick exists”
    • A powerful Eureka Moment is waiting for you! – the antipation is motivating (Visually hide a key prop would likewise make sense, the trick is to find it out, search the area closely)

Cultivate the Trust of The Solver

  • 前期出现的后期谜题需要有明确的标识————告诉玩家他缺少什么道具， 否则玩家会尝试解谜，然后无功而返————这会让游戏的谜题失去玩家的信任
  • A solution in Superland is to tell player that the puzzle is solvable, but it’s sort of unsurprising and seems intentional.

Avoid Cheap Tricks!

  • Puzzle is supported to be natural rather than making cunning obstacles
  • Do not make player do lots of searching without a clue – 比如说游戏中经常有大量的可破坏的箱子，而且这些箱子大概率不会掉出东西，或者只有一些非常微小的奖励(以至于完全可以无视掉)。那么，在一般情况下，就不应该把重要的道具放在这些箱子里，来强迫玩家“必须做一些本来无意义的事”才能破解谜题。同样的，在一个非常刁钻隐蔽的位置藏匿关键道具也是同理
  • 游戏的谜题风格需要在一定程度上保持统一，太多的不一致和变化很难建立玩家对规则的认知

Too much changing rules bring inconsistency, then player will never know whether they can expect their understanding of the game and their Conceptual Model for how the game systems work to be correct.

Be Careful With Intransparencies

  • Intransparencies – Aspects of a puzzle that are not explained to the solver. Such as hidden goals, hidden rulesm,hidden affordance(隐藏的能力), hidden interactions, hidden consequences…
  • Intransparencies may make inability to perceive or interpret the state of the puzzle

Faciliate Experimentation

  • Experimentation makes intransparencies transparent – show the hidden rules
  • Avoid making experimentation frustrating and monotonous
  • Get the solver on the right path. Confirmers – When player put a correct thing, give feedback(green light or sound)to remind players that they have done a right thing and keep going on!
  • Red herrings(红鲱鱼：指转移注意力的事物) Types:
    • Dreesings: needless non-pertinent decorations that are salient enough to be interpreted as pertinent(环境搭建，视觉引导，突出谜题，本身与谜题无关)
    • Distractors: affordances that are not needed
    • Fake puzzles and ghost puzzles: false clues mistaken as puzzles…
    • Obfuscation and misdirection: dead end approach(死胡同方法), get player a negative confirmer(but do not make too much trial cost)
    • Rabbit trails
      • Solver may only change approach after giving up
      • Seal off rabbit trials, add negative confirmer

Match Pertinence with salience.
让相关性与显著性相匹配。相关性是指与谜题有关的东西————线索、交互、角色能力、物品……；显著性是指事物被玩家感知的强度。

Seek Depth in Systems and Ruleset(追求系统与规则集的深度)

  • Get player form Conceptual Model(概念模型)
  • Capacity of different puzzle types(谜题的容量): 游戏的机制所支持的不同样式谜题的上限 —— 基于目前的机制，我们可以设计出多少种谜题？是否需要加入新的机制？如何设计新的机制？新加入的机制能够带来多少新的谜题？
  • Where does depth come form
    • Go puzzles(天然的内在深度): 简单的基本规则构建出复杂而多变的结果，通常是基于数学或自然科学的。围棋。
    • Contrivance(人为构造): 人为构造的(复杂)规则。炉石传说。
    • Interactions(规则互动):n个不同规则，就有2^n种组合方式。 Baby is You， 塞尔达， 原神(元素反应)
    • Level geometry(关卡几何结构): 空间带来的多样性。
      • connectivity/path
      • packing or covering
      • perspective, line of sight
      • logic gates
      • state manipulation

Understand Your Puzzle’s State Space and Path to Solution

  • State Space: 谜题可能存在的状态————初始状态-中间状态-完成状态

  • in：谜题的入口

  • out：谜题的出口

  • 圆：状态空间(state space)

  • 圆之间的连线：可能的转换路径

  • Battlenecks(瓶颈): 状态空间的转化路径非常少

  • Difficuly Dimension：谜题难度的控制

    • 状态空间越多，谜题越难
    • 瓶颈越多，谜题越南

  • Path to Solution

    • 状态空间之间的转换越多(more paths to solution)，解谜过程中的可能性越多
    • 瓶颈少，状态空间转换多的谜题不容易让玩家感觉到被卡住
    • Steps: 入口到出口经历的状态空间————谜题的长度(需要的步数)

Speak palyfully to The Solver

Computer-generated Sudoku Puzzles are lacking a vital ingredient that make puzzles enjoyable - the sense of communication between solver and author.
– Nobuhiko Kanamoto

  • Form a pattern/motif (形成模式/主题)
  • Repeated use a lemma/pattern (规则的复用)
  • Ladder or Chain repetition (阶梯式/递进)
  • Variant or twist on lemma (转折)
  • Setup and payoff (反馈与结果)
  • Clever surprise ending (尤里卡时刻)

正如林田宏一的“关卡设计四步法”一样，起承转合————引入-发展-转折-总结。这是一种非常自然，让人非常舒适的体验，而计算机生成的谜题很难有这种效果。

Design from the Front (Forward Design, 正向设计)

  • Design from the front: Placing clues in the order that the player would follow when solving the puzzle.
  • Quiet control over the solution experience – diffculty, path and flow, deductive solution…
  • Make the Key Move provably unique – 正向设计中推进谜题的”关键一步”往往具有唯一性，如果存在设计者未曾想到的其他解法，很有可能让玩家错过精心设计的Eureka moment – 这在电子游戏中时常发生

所以，许多类型的谜题，如象棋谜题、围棋谜题，等大多逻辑推理谜题，都需要独一无二的解法来保护谜题精彩的演绎过程和独特的尤里卡时刻。
生化危机系列中基于叙事的解谜基本上都是这类型————每个物品都对应着特定的用途，形成Lock & Key的逻辑链，并以此来控制游戏流程。这种设计确保了玩家能够很好的理解设计者的想法，在游戏过程中与设计者交流，并在设计者的”指导”下遵循既定的节奏、流程去探索、体验故事。

  • Frequenctly have to coerce player toward the intended solution

Add a Theme

  • Theme – get puzzle more meemorable and identifiable
  • Additional sparkle 特别的尤里卡时刻
  • Aesthetic value 美学价值
  • Easier to descaibe and reason about “符合直觉”
  • Avoid blank page syndrome 避免”白纸综合征”————“平平无奇”“过眼云烟”

Do Not Be Clever in Ways That The Solver Will Never Notice

  • Don’t get player feeling weird
  • Make sure your design according with intuition and common sense 符合直觉、符合尝试的设计

The Format Changes Everything

  • Paper/physical/screen
  • Mobile/Desktop/TV
  • Controller/keyboard/touch
  • Undo/restart/savestate/hint/note-taking system
  • Punishment of mistakes/lose

Break the Rules, Sometimes Perversely

  • Another way to create Eureka moment

Peroration

  • Serendipity Occurs When You’re Actively Searching for Good Ideas
  • Notice your own Eureka momnents and turn them into puzzles
